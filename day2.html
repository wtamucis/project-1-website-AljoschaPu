Day Two of the Texas MS150 begins in the quiet early morning hours as riders roll out of La Grange, still carrying the pride and fatigue from the previous day’s nearly 100 miles. The start is filled with an atmosphere of determination as cyclists gather in the cool air, headlights and taillights glowing against the dawn. The route eases out of town, taking riders up and out of the Colorado River Valley. This part of the ride is both peaceful and energizing, with the sun rising over open farmland and wooded stretches that remind everyone of the beauty of rural Texas. The first miles of the day allow participants to settle back into their rhythm, connect with teammates, and focus on the goal that lies ahead.

As the morning progresses, the route unfolds into rolling hills and broad ranchland that test the legs and reward the eyes. Riders pass fields dotted with grazing cattle, wildflowers blooming in the spring, and long stretches of quiet country roads where the only sounds are bicycle tires humming and birds overhead. The highlight of the day’s first half is the lunch stop on the shore of Lake Somerville. Here, cyclists can take a longer break to refuel while enjoying the sparkling water and cool breezes off the lake. The natural setting makes this stop more than just a meal break as it becomes a moment to relax, take in the scenery, and gather strength for the miles ahead. The camaraderie at Lake Somerville is palpable, as riders share stories, encouragement, and the shared excitement of nearing the finish.

From the lake, the journey turns toward College Station, and anticipation builds with every passing mile. The terrain shifts once more, with a mix of gentle climbs and fast descents leading riders closer to the city. As cyclists approach Texas A&M University, the energy changes as the quiet country roads give way to cheering crowds, banners, and the buzz of a festival atmosphere. The grand finish at Kyle Field is unlike any other moment on the ride. Thousands of supporters line the arrival area, music fills the air, and teammates cross the finish line together in triumph. The sight of the stadium, the sound of the crowd, and the feeling of accomplishment transform the end of the ride into a celebration of resilience and community. More than just the conclusion of 150 miles, the finish at Kyle Field stands as a powerful reminder of the shared mission to fight multiple sclerosis and the strength that comes from riding together.
